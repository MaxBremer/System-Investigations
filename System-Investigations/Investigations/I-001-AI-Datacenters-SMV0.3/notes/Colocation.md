
## Definition
The practice of renting space from a third-party company in their datacenter instead of constructing/owning your own costly facility.
In a nutshell, this is just a fancy way of referring to renting, instead of buying, when it comes to space and basic resources within a data center.
Importantly, it seems that typically you bring your own hardware. You're only paying for:
- the physical space
- the power
- the connectivity
- the security

---

## Why It Exists
Because owning and operating your own datacenter is expensive, and potentially overkill. Same goes for expanding your existing datacenter(s). 

---

## Example
HPE offers a variety of colocation products to its customers.

---

## Why It Matters
This is likely the option that most players would take. Only the big boys can really afford their own full-on datacenters.
Furthermore, odds are that many of the ongoing data center investments aren't actually to use themselves (though some likely are), but to lease out space to others.
