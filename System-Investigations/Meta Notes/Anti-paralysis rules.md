Rules whose idea is to prevent analysis paralysis, stopping points, and generally bad habits that halt me from progressing.
- Map before mastering; you do not need to understand the actors before placing them provisionally on a map.
- When the system feels too large, follow one unit through it.
- Sample before surveying; understand one representative example before trying to catalog the industry.
- Questions must imply actions: "Who are the players?" does not imply a source.
  "Who owns and operates the facility used by a hyperscaler?" does
- Limit reconnaissance: Three overview sources, ten questions, one diagram. Then select a thread.
- Preserve uncertainty: Write "I currently group these actors as..." not "The industry contains exactly these categories"
- Defer aggressively: A question about transformer manufacturing can be recorded without being answered now. Stop at the boundary.
